Frauke Petry

Frauke Petry was the leader of the Alternative for Germany (AfD) party, whose political platform is described as nationalist and right-wing populist. Petry has made many islamophobic comments as a political figure, such as “the immigration of Muslims will change our culture.” She also believes that women should not wear burqas in schools, stating “this sort of religious costume should not be worn.” The AfD also displays posters reading “Islam doesn't belong to Germany" and "Freedom of the woman is not negotiable!" Petry and the AfD’s position suggests that women cannot be free when wearing burqas and that the garment is seen as backwards.

Beatrix von Storch

Beatrix von Storch is German politician who made anti-Islam remarks. She is a member of the Bundestag (federal parliament) and was a member of the European Parliament. Von Storch is a member of the AfD who had said “Islam is a political ideology that is not compatible with the German Constitution,” and went on to support the banning of minarets, burqas, and mosque calls to prayer.

André Poggenburg

André Poggenburg is another far-right politician who made anti-Islam and anti-immigrant comments. He founded the far-right Awakening of German Patriots (AdP) party, and called Turks “camel drivers” and immigrants with dual nationality a “homeless mob we no longer want to have.”
