Islamophobia in Advertisements

Islamophobie in der Werbung

TO DO

TO DO

In the eve of the 2017 federal elections in Germany, the far-right Alternative for Germany (AfD) party launched an ad campaign focusing on misogyny and Islamophobia. One of the ads featured two women in bikinis, with the text “Burqas? We prefer bikinis.” Another featured an image of a baby pig with the caption “Islam? It doesn’t fit in with our cuisine.” The ads were displayed on the streets of Berlin in order to galvanize support for far-right anti-immigrant and anti-Islam policies. The ads were utterly islamophobic and stoked up fear of Muslim migrants and refugees in Germany.

In 2017, an AfD ad depicting bloody tiremarks spread among a wide German audience on Facebook and Twitter. The ad included the words “the tracks left by the world chancellor in Europe” along with numbers of deaths from terror attacks. This ad placed the blame of these deaths on Chancellor Angela Merkel’s 2015 open-door policies for refugees. Clearly, this ad was used to demonize the Muslim community and to unify right-wing supporters with AfD.
