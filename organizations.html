Organizations Countering Islamophobia

Organisationen gegen Islamophobie

JUMA

In Germany, the Muslim youth group JUMA (Young, Muslim, Active) aims to give young Muslims “a voice and makes their social commitment visible to the general public.” Members do so by sharing their perspectives through social discourse with politicians and journalists. The chairman of the board of Juma is Dennis Kirschbaum, a political educator and interfaith activist. He has been involved with JUMA since its initiation in 2010 by Berlin Senate and RAA (Regional Centre for Education, Integration and Democracy) Berlin. Sponsors of JUMA include Robert Bosch Stiftung, BPB (Federal Agency for Civic Education), and RAA Berlin. Each of these sponsors are focused on social activism, therefore, aligning with the activism done by the young members of JUMA. For example, JUMA brings their perspectives through professional public events, interviews, and blogs regarding refugees, interreligious dialogue, and Islamophobia in education. This group also provides opportunities for members to become peer trainers for interreligious dialogue. With this skill, these young members share their understanding that use of any religion for violence is not compatible with their religious identities. JUMA places a large emphasis on unifying young Muslims despite how they live their faith and does not consider itself a religious organization. Perhaps this is why Muslim funding sources are not listed for this organization.

Central Council of Muslims in Germany

The Central Council of Muslims in Germany is an organization that aims to counter Islamophobia. The organization’s goal is to represent the multi-ethnic Muslim community in Germany. It does not seek to replace mosque communities and Islamic associations. It also hopes to engage in dialogue about Muslim affairs with the German government and other organizations. The Central Council of Muslims in Germany is funded by membership fees, fundraising in mosques, and private donations. The organization’s president, Aiman Mazyek has criticized the rightwing populist Alternative for Germany (AfD) of “riding a wave of Islamophobia.” He said the AfD is “the first time since Hitler's Germany that there is a party which discredits and existentially threatens an entire religious community.” In response to growing Islamophobia in Germany, Mazyek called on the government to appoint an Islamophobia commissioner in 2019. The organization has also advocated for more police presence around mosques in order to deter attacks by extremists.

Islamic Community Millî Görüs (ICMG)

The Islamic Community Millî Görüs (ICMG) is an organization that gives comprehensive religious, social, and cultural services in Germany. The organization is solely funded by its members and has close ties to Turkey’s ruling AKP party. The IGMG participates in Open Mosque Day to allow the public to learn more about Islam, with the aim of eliminating biases against Islam held by portions of the public. In Germany, the ICMG has about 30,000 members and 300 mosques and prayer rooms. The organization is also active in fighting against discrimination. It documents discrimination towards Muslims and deals with discrimination cases. ICMG portrays hate crimes in Germany as symbolic of Islamophobia of German society. They are often accused of being a radical and extremist group by the German intelligence agency “for promoting the creation and expansion of Islamist elements in Germany.”
